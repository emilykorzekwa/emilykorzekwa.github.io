Saint Ambrose

Saint Ambrose was a bishop of Milan in the 4th century. The honeycomb pattern in his halo alludes to his historical association with bees. He carries a staff and wears a mitre to show that he was a bishop. Although there is little historical evidence of whether or not bishops wore the mitre (hat) in the 4th century, I decided to include it because it would more clearly communicate to modern viewers that he was a bishop, and not just a priest.

Colored pencil and ink on bristol paper

8 in X 10 in
